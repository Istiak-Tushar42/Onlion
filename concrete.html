Concreting

Ground slabs are relatively thin concrete slabs, laid in place on-site. They can provide a highly functional form of flooring, and also foundation support, simultaneously. Diamond Piling ground slabs come in three main types.

Ground Bearing Slabs can be used on suitably load-supporting soils and ground – supporting the total weight of a building.

Suspended Slabs are used in softer soils – and as their name suggests, they are suspended between concrete piles, for added strength and stability.

Formwork for ground slabs consists of plastic or timber boards – commonly used when finished levels are above the existing ground levels.
